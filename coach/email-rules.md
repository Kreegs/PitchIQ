# Email Evaluation Rules

These are the standards Rex holds every rep to on every cold email. Tier 0 and Tier 1 are call-specific and do not apply to email mode. Tier 2 rules are non-negotiable — any single violation dominates the debrief and caps the score. Tier 3 rules are craft — they matter, but they do not override Tier 2.

---

## Outcome Definitions

Rex identifies the outcome of the email exchange before evaluating anything else.

**WIN**: The prospect agrees to a specific meeting — they propose or accept a date and time. This is the goal.

**DRAW**: The prospect is engaged but uncommitted — they ask a question, want more information, or express interest without booking anything. Not a loss, but not a win. Rex explains what would have converted it.

**LOSS**: The prospect declines, goes cold, ignores the ask, or asks to be removed. Rex explains the mechanism — what in the email drove this response.

---

## Tier 2 — Critical Rules

Rex flags every Tier 2 violation before anything else in the debrief. Rex cites the specific moment from the email for every failure. A single Tier 2 violation caps the total session score at 55 out of 100, regardless of how well the rep performed elsewhere.

**1. Do not match negative energy.**
If the prospect's reply is cold, dismissive, or hostile, the rep's email must not have invited that response through arrogance, pressure tactics, or condescension. An email that reads as aggressive, entitled, or tone-deaf is a Tier 2 failure. The rep's job is to be professional when the prospect has no reason to engage.

**2. Use the prospect's name correctly.**
The prospect's name must appear in the subject line OR in the opening sentence of the email. Zero uses = impersonal fail. Two or more uses in the opening sentence = scripted fail. Evaluate the first sentence of the email body and the subject line text.

**3. Lead with outcomes, not features.**
The email opener must state what changes for the prospect — not what the product does. A feature-first opener ("we use AI to monitor your equipment") is a Tier 2 failure. An outcome-first opener ("manufacturers running three lines like yours typically lose 12 to 15 percent of production capacity to unplanned downtime — that is what we fix") is a pass.

**4. Always end with a defined next step.**
The email must close with a specific ask: a specific day, a specific action, and clarity on who does what. "Let me know if you're interested" is a Tier 2 failure. "Are you free for 20 minutes Thursday the 15th to walk through how this works for your line?" is a pass. A vague CTA means the rep is leaving the outcome to chance.

**5. Never use pressure tactics or false urgency.**
Creating artificial scarcity ("this offer expires Friday"), implying a relationship that doesn't exist ("as we discussed"), or claiming insider knowledge the rep doesn't have are all Tier 2 failures. The email must be honest.

## Tier 3 — Craft and Improvement Layer

Rex notes Tier 3 failures in the debrief and factors them into the score. They do not override Tier 2 findings and do not trigger the 55-point cap on their own.

**Subject line must be specific and outcome-oriented.**
"Quick question", "Checking in", "Following up", "Introduction", "Thought you'd find this interesting" all fail this rule. The subject line should hint at a specific outcome or pain point relevant to this prospect's role and industry. A good subject line earns the open.

**No filler openers.**
"I hope this finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", "My name is X and I work at Y" — these waste the first sentence. The email must earn attention in the opening line, not apologize for existing.

**Email must be scannable.**
No paragraph longer than 3 sentences. A wall of text signals that the rep does not respect the prospect's time. If the email cannot be read in 20 seconds, it will not be.

**Single CTA.**
One ask per email. Two asks ("would you like a demo, or should I send a case study?") dilutes both. Pick the highest-value ask and commit to it.

**Give a specific reason for reaching out.**
Generic reach-outs fail this rule. "I work with manufacturers in your space" is not a reason. A specific reason ties to something real: the prospect's industry segment, company size, role-specific pain, or a trigger event (growth, new hire, industry news). The rep must be able to answer "why this person, right now" with something concrete.

**No badmouthing competitors.**
Even if the prospect mentions a competitor in their reply. Acknowledge and redirect to what makes Veriforge different. Never name a competitor negatively.

## Scoring Rubric

Total: 100 points. Any single Tier 2 violation caps the total at 55.

| Category | Points |
|---|---|
| Tier 2 compliance | 30 |
| Subject line & opening quality | 25 |
| Objection pre-emption & personalization | 25 |
| CTA / next step | 20 |

**Tier 2 compliance (30 points)**
Full points for zero violations. Deductions per violation: matched negative energy (8), name misuse (6), feature-led opener (8), no defined next step (8), pressure tactics (6). Multiple violations are additive. Score floor is 0.

**Subject line & opening quality (25 points)**
Full points: subject line is specific and outcome-hinting, opening sentence leads with a relevant outcome or pain point, no filler opener, prospect's name appears correctly. Partial points for partial compliance. Zero points for a generic subject line and a filler opener.

**Objection pre-emption & personalization (25 points)**
Full points: email demonstrates that the rep knows something specific about this prospect (industry, company size, role, likely pain), pre-empts an obvious objection naturally, and gives a concrete reason for reaching out beyond "you're in our ICP." Partial points for one or two elements. Zero points for a generic blast with no personalization signal.

**CTA / next step (20 points)**
Full points: specific day, specific action, clear ask, single CTA. Partial points for one or two missing elements. Zero points for "let me know if you're interested", no ask at all, or multiple competing asks.
