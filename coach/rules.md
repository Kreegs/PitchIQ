# Evaluation Rules

These are the standards Rex holds every rep to on every call and email. Tier 0 rules are hard stops — any violation ends the call immediately. Tier 1 rules result in an automatic score of zero, but the call continues and violations are addressed in debrief. Tier 2 rules are non-negotiable — any single violation dominates the debrief and caps the score. Tier 3 rules are craft — they matter, but they do not override Tier 2.

---

## Tier 0 — Hard Stops

These are call-ending violations. If Rex identifies a Tier 0 violation, the call ends immediately, the debrief leads with it, and the session score is zero. No other rules are evaluated. There is no partial credit and no context that makes a Tier 0 violation acceptable.

**1. No profanity or cussing.**
The rep may not use profanity at any point during the call, regardless of tone, intent, or what the prospect said. A single instance is an immediate Tier 0 failure. This includes mild expletives and any language that would be inappropriate in a professional sales context.

---

## Tier 1 — Zero-Score Violations

Rex flags every Tier 1 violation at the top of the debrief. A Tier 1 violation results in an automatic session score of zero. The call is not ended, but the violation is the lead item in the debrief and no other sections are evaluated favorably. There is no context that makes a Tier 1 violation acceptable.

**1. No rudeness or disrespect toward the prospect.**
The rep may not speak to the prospect in a condescending, dismissive, or hostile manner under any circumstances. If the prospect is difficult, the rep's job is to stay professional — not match or escalate. Any instance of the rep being openly rude, sarcastic in a cutting way, or disrespectful is a Tier 1 failure.

---

## Tier 2 — Critical Rules

Rex flags every Tier 2 violation before anything else in the debrief. Rex cites the specific moment from the transcript for every failure. A single Tier 2 violation caps the total session score at 60 out of 100, regardless of how well the rep performed elsewhere.

**1. Never interrupt the prospect.**
Let silence sit. Dead air is not the rep's problem to fill. If the prospect is mid-sentence and the rep speaks, that is an interruption. It is a Tier 2 failure regardless of what the rep was about to say.

**2. Do not match negative energy.**
If the prospect is rude, dismissive, or hostile, the rep stays measured. Matching aggression, getting defensive, going cold, or snapping back are all Tier 2 failures. The rep's job is to stay professional when the prospect makes it hard.

**3. Use the prospect's name exactly once in the first two sentences.**
Not zero times — it reads as impersonal. Not twice — it reads as a script. Exactly once, in the opening exchange. Using the name correctly is a Tier 2 requirement, not a courtesy.

To evaluate this rule: find the rep's very first turn in the transcript and quote it exactly. Then count how many times the prospect's name appears in the first two sentences of that turn. The name appearing as the very first word of the opener — e.g. "Jenna, thank you for taking my call" — counts as correct usage. Pass if the name appears exactly once. Fail if it appears zero times or more than twice. Do not render a verdict before quoting the text.

**4. Lead with outcomes, not features.**
The opener must state what changes for the prospect — not what the product does. A feature-first opener ("we use AI to monitor your equipment") is a Tier 2 failure. An outcome-first opener ("manufacturers running three lines like yours typically lose 12 to 15 percent of production capacity to unplanned downtime — that is what we fix") is a pass.

**5. Three strikes rule.**
If the same objection surfaces three times in a single call, the rep must change approach on the third instance. Repeating the same response with different words or louder emphasis is a Tier 2 failure on the third strike. A change of approach means asking a new diagnostic question, reframing the conversation entirely, or conceding and pivoting to an exit.

**6. Always end with a defined next step.**
The call or email must end with a specific date, a specific action, and clarity on who is responsible for what. "I'll follow up" is a Tier 2 failure. "I'll send the IT security brief by Thursday — can we put thirty minutes on the calendar for the 14th, you and your operations lead?" is a pass. The next step must be agreed to by the prospect, not just stated by the rep.

## Tier 3 — Craft and Improvement Layer

Rex notes Tier 3 failures in the debrief and factors them into the score. They do not override Tier 2 findings and do not trigger the 60-point cap on their own.

**No filler words.**
Um, uh, like, you know, sort of, kind of, honestly, basically, right. Each instance is noted. Multiple instances in a single exchange are flagged as a pattern, not individually counted.

**Give a specific reason for the call.**
Generic openers fail this rule. "I'm reaching out because we work with manufacturers" is not a reason. A specific reason ties to something real: the prospect's industry, their company size, a pain point relevant to their role. The rep must be able to answer "why this person, on this call, today" with something other than "because they are in our ICP."

**Never ask "is this a bad time?"**
It offers the prospect an exit before the conversation has a chance to start. Any variation — "did I catch you at a bad time," "is now okay," "do you have a minute" — fails this rule.

**Never badmouth competitors.**
Even if the prospect invites it. If a competitor is brought up, acknowledge it and redirect to what Veriforge does differently. Never name a competitor in a negative context.

**Never quote price unprompted.**
Price comes after value is established. If the rep mentions a number before the prospect asks, it is a Tier 3 failure. If the prospect asks for price and the rep answers with context, that is not a violation.

**Acknowledge objections before answering them.**
The rep must show they heard the objection before responding to it. Barreling through — restating the pitch immediately after an objection — fails this rule. "That makes sense, but..." also fails it. The acknowledgment must be genuine before the reframe begins.

**Never argue.**
If the prospect pushes back, the rep reframes — they do not dig in and defend. Arguing means repeating the same point with more force or more frustration. Reframing means finding a new angle, asking a question, or conceding the prospect's point and pivoting.

**Leave the prospect with something they agreed to do.**
Even if no next step was formally booked, the call or email should end with some form of commitment from the prospect. A vague "sounds good, send it over" with no specifics is a Tier 3 failure if it is the best the rep achieved.

## Scoring Rubric

Total: 100 points. Any single Tier 2 violation caps the total at 60. A Tier 1 violation results in a score of zero. A Tier 0 violation ends the call and results in a score of zero.

| Category | Points |
|---|---|
| Tier 2 compliance | 40 |
| Opening quality | 15 |
| Objection handling | 25 |
| Close / next step | 20 |

**Tier 2 compliance (40 points)**
Full points for zero violations. Deductions per violation: interrupting (10), matched energy (10), name misuse (8), feature-led opener (10), three strikes failure (6), no defined next step (10). Multiple violations are additive. Score floor is 0.

**Opening quality (15 points)**
Full points: outcome-led, specific reason for the call, prospect's name used correctly, no "is this a bad time." Partial points for partial compliance. Zero points for a feature-first opener with a generic reason for calling.

**Objection handling (25 points)**
Full points: every objection acknowledged before responding, no arguing, Acknowledge-Reframe applied correctly. Partial points for inconsistent application. Zero points for barreling through every objection or matching hostility.

**Close / next step (20 points)**
Full points: specific date, specific action, responsible party named, prospect agreed. Partial points for one or two missing elements. Zero points for "I'll follow up" or no closing attempt.
